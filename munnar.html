Munnar - Your window to Kerela's natural beauty

Nestled in the lush embrace of Kerala’s Western Ghats, Munnar is a hill station that feels like a serene painting brought to life. Famous for its endless tea plantations, mist-covered valleys, and rolling hills, Munnar sits at around 1,600 meters (5,200 ft) above sea level. The journey usually begins from Kochi (Cochin), which is the nearest major city and airport—about 130 km away. The drive from Kochi to Munnar, although winding and steep in parts, offers scenic views of waterfalls, spice gardens, and charming hillside villages. The roads are well-maintained but do require patience due to their curves and elevation, especially for first-time drivers in hilly regions.

Munnar is more than just a postcard destination—it’s an experience. While most of the main town is easy to explore on foot or by vehicle, trekking spots like Meesapulimala, Echo Point, and Top Station range from moderate to challenging difficulty. Meesapulimala, for instance, is the second-highest peak in the Western Ghats and demands a decent level of fitness and guided trekking. For those seeking a calm, less physically demanding trip, there are plenty of tea museums, botanical gardens, and scenic viewpoints accessible by road. Whether you're an adventurer or a peace-seeker, Munnar has a flavor of nature and charm for every kind of traveler.

Safety & Travel Tips

• Drive Carefully on Hills:- Roads are steep and winding—go slow or hire a local driver if needed.
• Check Weather Beforehand:- Munnar can be rainy or foggy—carry light rainwear and check the forecast.
• Start Early for Treks:- Begin treks in the morning and use a local guide for tougher routes.
• Carry Cash:- Some remote areas don’t accept digital payments—keep enough cash on hand.
• Stay Hydrated and Layered:- Weather changes fast—wear layers and carry water during outings.
• Respect Nature and Local Customs:- Stick to marked paths, avoid littering, and respect local culture.

How to get there?

Munnar is accessible year-round, with scenic routes offering a memorable journey through Kerala’s lush landscapes.

BY AIR

Nearest airport is Cochi International Airport (130 km). Taxis and buses are available from there.

BY TRAIN

The closest major railway station is Aluva (110 km), with Ernakulam Junction (130 km) as another option. From either station, cabs and buses connect to Munnar.

BY CAR

Munnar is well-connected by road from Kochi, Madurai, and other nearby cities. Regular buses, private taxis, and rental cars are available.
