Overview

Varkala Beach, also known as Papanasam Beach, is a serene coastal destination located in the Thiruvananthapuram district of Kerala, India. It is famed for its stunning red laterite cliffs that stand dramatically next to the Arabian Sea, creating a unique landscape. The beach is not only a hub for sunbathing and swimming but also holds spiritual significance due to the belief that a dip in its waters washes away sins. A natural spring emerging from the cliffs is considered holy, and many pilgrims visit the beach for rituals. Apart from its natural beauty, Varkala offers a calm, peaceful atmosphere compared to the more commercial beaches like Kovalam. The sunsets are particularly mesmerizing, attracting photographers and nature lovers alike. The long stretch of golden sand is dotted with cafes, shops, and yoga centers catering to both domestic and international travelers.

Tourism in Varkala is vibrant yet not overcrowded, making it ideal for a relaxing getaway. Visitors often enjoy Ayurvedic treatments, meditation sessions, and traditional Kerala cuisine with a sea view. Adventure lovers can explore paragliding, surfing, and trekking trails around the cliffs. The blend of spiritual charm and beach culture makes Varkala unique among Indian coastal towns.Whether for wellness, adventure, or tranquility, Varkala Beach remains a cherished destination on Kerala’s tourism map.

Safety & Travel Tips

1.Swim in Designated Areas – Always swim in marked zones where lifeguards are present, as currents can be strong.
2.Avoid Late-Night Swims – The sea can be unpredictable at night, and visibility is poor.
3.Secure Your Belongings – Keep valuables in your hotel or use anti-theft bags while on the beach.
4.Stay Hydrated – Carry water, especially if you're out in the sun for long hours.
5.Respect Local Customs – Wear appropriate clothing when away from the beach and be polite with locals.
6.Use Trusted Transport – Opt for registered taxis or scooters from verified rental shops for safety.

Culture & Cuisine

Kerala’s culture is a vibrant blend of ancient traditions, classical art forms, and religious harmony. The state is known for its colorful festivals like Onam, Vishu, and Thrissur Pooram, which showcase traditional music, dance, and temple rituals. Classical art forms such as Kathakali, Mohiniyattam, and Theyam reflect its rich heritage. The cuisine of Kerala is diverse, featuring coconut, rice, and spices as staples. Signature dishes include appam with stew, puttu and kadala curry, and the famous Kerala Sadya served on a banana leaf. Seafood lovers can relish fresh fish curry and prawn dishes cooked with local flavors. Kerala’s culture and cuisine together offer a deeply rooted and flavorful experience for every visitor.

How to get there?

Varkala Beach is a serene coastal destination in Kerala. It is reachable via air, rail, and road with good connectivity. Plan your trip with the route that suits your travel needs.

BY AIR

Trivandrum International Airport is about 40 km from Varkala.

BY TRAIN

Varkala Sivagiri Railway Station is the closest, just 2 km away.

BY CAR

Frequent buses and taxis connect Varkala with nearby cities.
