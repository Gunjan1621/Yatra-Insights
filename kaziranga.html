Kaziranga: Where the Wild Roam Free

Nestled in the heart of Assam, Kaziranga National Park is one of India’s finest wildlife sanctuaries and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It’s world-renowned for being home to the largest population of the endangered one-horned rhinoceros, alongside majestic elephants, wild water buffalo, swamp deer, and a thriving tiger population. With its floodplains, tall elephant grasses, and rich biodiversity, Kaziranga offers an unforgettable safari experience—whether by jeep or on the back of an elephant.
The park spans over 800 sq km and is split into four main zones—each offering unique sightings and terrain. It’s not just a place for wildlife lovers, but for anyone seeking raw, untouched nature. The silence of the forest, broken by the call of a hornbill or a distant roar, makes Kaziranga a true wilderness escape in Northeast India. It also serves as a critical ecological haven, protecting migratory birds and aquatic species along the mighty Brahmaputra River. Early mornings in Kaziranga are magical—with golden mist hanging low over the grasslands, it's nature at its most poetic.

Know Before You Go: Tips for a Smooth Kaziranga Trip

• Book Safaris Early:- Entry is limited, so book jeep or elephant safaris in advance, especially in peak season (Nov–Apr).
• Dress Earthy and Covered:- Wear muted colors like green or brown and full sleeves to blend in with the jungle and protect against insects.
• Follow the Naturalist's Instructions:- Don’t step out of the safari vehicle and always listen to your guide—they know how to keep you safe and improve your chances of wildlife sightings.
• Carry Essentials:- Pack water, sunscreen, a hat, and binoculars. Mobile networks can be patchy, so download offline maps if needed.
• Respect the Wildlife:- Don’t make noise or try to attract animals. Maintain a respectful distance and never litter inside the park.
• Plan Around Weather:- Avoid monsoon months (May to October) when the park is closed due to flooding. Travel in winter or early spring for the best experience.

Culture & Cuisine

Kaziranga isn’t just about wildlife—it’s also a window into the rich Assamese culture that surrounds it. The nearby villages and towns reflect a simple yet vibrant lifestyle, with traditional bamboo homes, handwoven textiles, and heartfelt hospitality. Festivals like Bihu bring people together through music, dance, and food, creating moments that feel both intimate and celebratory. The local cuisine is earthy and nourishing, built around seasonal produce, rice, and freshwater fish. Signature dishes include Masor Tenga, Khar, and a variety of slow-cooked meat and lentil preparations. One delicacy that stands out is the traditional Pitha—a sweet or savory rice cake often steamed or pan-roasted during festivals. These are lovingly handmade and symbolize the region’s warmth and connection to tradition. Wrap up your day with a hot cup of Assam tea, best enjoyed with a view of the setting sun over the lush, wild landscape.

How to get there?

Reaching Kaziranga is easy via a combination of air, rail, and road:

BY AIR

The nearest airport is Jorhat Airport (approx. 97 km) or Guwahati Airport (approx. 217 km), both connected to major Indian cities. Taxis and shared cabs are available from both airports.

BY TRAIN

The closest railway station is Furkating Junction, about 75 km from the park. From there, hire a cab or take a bus to Kaziranga.

BY CAR

Kaziranga is well-connected by road from Guwahati, Tezpur, and Jorhat. You can hire a taxi or use government/private buses that run regularly.
