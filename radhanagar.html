Overview

Radhanagar Beach, located on Havelock Island in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, is known for its pristine beauty and clear blue waters. It has been consistently ranked among Asiaâ€™s top beaches, making it a popular destination for both domestic and international tourists. The beach stretches over a long expanse, offering plenty of space for sunbathing, swimming, and beach sports. The soft, white sand and the lush tropical surroundings create a perfect setting for relaxation and unwinding. The crystal-clear water and gentle waves make it ideal for a peaceful swim or a serene walk along the shore. The beach is also renowned for its breathtaking sunsets, with the sun dipping below the horizon, painting the sky in vibrant hues. It's an excellent spot for photography, capturing the stunning vistas that change with the time of day.

Beyond its scenic beauty, Radhanagar Beach is also an eco-friendly destination, where visitors are encouraged to respect the environment. The area is home to diverse marine life, making it a great spot for snorkelling and other water-based activities. However, as the beach is relatively untouched, visitors are advised to maintain its natural charm by avoiding littering and disturbing wildlife. The beach's calm atmosphere is perfect for those seeking solitude, while also being an excellent place for families.

Safety & Travel Tips

1.Swimming Safety: Always swim within the designated safe zones and avoid swimming after sunset.
2.Currents and Weather: Be cautious of strong currents, especially during the monsoon season, and check weather updates before visiting.
3.Best Time to Visit: Visit early in the morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds and enjoy cooler weather.
4.Child Safety: Keep an eye on children at all times, and ensure they stay within shallow waters.
5.Sun Protection & Belongings: Use reef-safe sunscreen to protect your skin and the environment, and bring a waterproof bag for your belongings.
6.Respect for Wildlife & Essentials: Respect local wildlife, stay hydrated, wear comfortable footwear, and carry cash as food options may be limited.

Culture & Cuisine

The culture of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands is a mix of indigenous traditions and influences from mainland India. Home to tribes like the Great Andamanese, Onge, and Nicobarese, the islands preserve unique customs, dances, and art. The local population also includes people from Tamil, Bengali, and Telugu communities, enriching the cultural fabric. The cuisine heavily features seafood, with dishes like fish curry, seafood barbecue, and coconut rice. The Nicobarese prepare meals based on rice and fish, while mainland influences add a variety of rice-based dishes and sweet treats. Local flavors blend spices and coconut, creating a diverse culinary experience. The fusion of cultures makes the food and traditions truly unique. Visitors can enjoy the islands' rich cultural and gastronomic offerings.

How to get there?

Reaching Radhanagar Beach is simple and scenic.It is accessible through air, ferry, and road connections.Travelers can pick the mode that suits their itinerary.

BY AIR

Fly to Veer Savarkar Airport in Port Blair, then travel by road and ferry.

BY TRAIN

Regular ferries run from Port Blair to Havelock Island.

BY CAR

From Havelock jetty, it's a short ride to the beach by cab or bus.
